# Fishing

Got another friend who is playing online, levelling up their first character. This is a story about sharing an experience even though our levels are far apart.

---

While playing World of Warcraft and streaming in Discord, levelling my Human Warrior all the way so far upto level 40 I have had various other people drop in, and become interested in playing some WoW too. While a lot of them are quite turned off by the subscription cost, the more I play the more convinced they seem to be to also give it a shot.

One such person decided to start their first time playing the game as a human Paladin, on a journey to join me and my priest friend for some of our dungeon runs and gameplay antics. At this time I had just also started a new horde character named Jakn that I had been levelling too, trying out a warlock for the first time (had hoped I would be able to name my demon imp, but alas, no Daxter minion for me, stuck with Zupuri instead haha).

I felt bad though leaving the human Paladin friend just levelling on his own, as if he were to hit the same level as our main characters there would still be a lot questing ahead of him.

> The journey back to Goldshire

With my hearthstone being currently set to Theramore Isle, in Dustwallow Marsh, I was quite far away from Goldshire, so started my journey back. Flying on a griffin, taking a boat, then another griffin, and arriving in Stormwind.

While walking out through the Stormwind gates, I try to hover over the peoples names as they walk through. As I was crossing the bridge and noticed my friends name pop up. I quickly did a U-Turn and called out to him (both in Discord and in game).

From that point onward I grouped up with him, and started helping him out with his questing. We took out Hogger, Princess, helped out Marshal Haggard, collected some logs and bested Prowlers and Bears.

Unfortunately, not much of an experience gain for me, and whenever I was grouped and nearby to him, he would gain only 2xp.

> Fishing time

He had to go on a bit of a break, so he set himself to follow me. While walking back to Goldshire, I remembered I had a fishing rod in my inventory, and was still only level 1, so I walked over to the pond inside the town and started fishing and levelling my skill. He came back, and seemed interested to also do some fishing.

I took him over to the fishing trainer, and bought him his own rod, then showed him the pond I was fishing at. It was now my turn to jump off for a bit, and cook dinner, so I sat next to the pond while he continuied.

After cooking, I check back in on him. Level 150 fishing already. WHAT! We figure out he needs to reach level 10 to then unlock more levels, and best next area to skill up is inside Stormwind (to also hopefully get a ring my priest friend told us about, with like a 1/1000 chance of fishing up).

As he is level 9 1/2 he goes off to kill some monsters, and I start skilling up my own fishing as well. I also learn that we can do cooking skilling to with the fish we catch, and find the recipe trainer for this is inside stormwind too.

> Shared Experience

What I have been enjoying most about this, is even though we are so different in our normal level (level 10 vs level 40), we are still able to share in a part of the game neither of us has had the chance to rank up yet, with him even getting ahead of me.

Hoping to continue levelling up my fishing with him, and trying out new locations as we progress. Who knows what we may catch!
