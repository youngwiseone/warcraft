# The Deadmines

Westfall always feels a little uneasy after dark. The fields look quiet from a distance, but every road seems to lead back to the Defias, and every farmer has another rumor about what is happening beneath the old mine.

## Entering the Mine

We stepped in at level 18, not because it was safe, but because it felt like the right moment to try. The first few pulls set the tone immediately. Goblin workers packed the tunnels, patrols overlapped at the worst times, and every careless charge threatened to drag in half the room.

The deeper we went, the more the dungeon felt like a real stronghold instead of a simple cave. Lantern light flickered off the timber supports, engineers barked from the back lines, and the mine started to feel like a living hideout instead of just another leveling stop.

## What I Remember Most

- The foundry area punished every bad pull.
- Mr. Smite made us slow down and respect the encounter.
- Captain Cookie turned the ending into pure chaos.

> The Deadmines is at its best when the group is slightly underleveled and every win feels earned.

## Why It Still Works

Classic dungeons are memorable when they make you feel like the world exists outside your character sheet. The Deadmines does that better than most early instances. You do not just clear mobs. You invade a rebellion's headquarters, descend into its machinery, and fight through it deck by deck until the whole run feels like a proper campaign.

We walked out with loot, repairs to make, and the kind of dungeon story that is still easy to retell later.
