# Whirlwind Axe

A short lead-up to the Whirlwind Axe quest, from hardcore memories and warrior advice to finally reaching level 30 and setting out for Ratchet.

---

The first time I heard of this axe was back when I started playing wow for the first time, straight into modded hardcore (yes, warrior modded hardcore was my first experience with WoW).

It was from a streamer who was running around Darkshire, levelling up so that he would be able to do the quest without dying. I had no idea what the axe looked like, I had no idea what it did, but everyone in the chat seemed to by hyped up for the power boost it would give this player.

> Hardcore - not for the weak of heart

Excited, I started levelling on my hardcore character/s. One hit lvl 15, then died upon reaching Westfall. Next Character, same thing, Next character this time hitting level 18 then dying to some defias while trying to get a pocket watch for a quest. Ouch. Finally got one character all the way up to level 22, but through my stupidity I followed a player who was being "helpful" and lead me into a cave of overlevelled syndicate.

I actually managed to get to a safe spot, but was stuck in the middle of the cave. Out of even more stupidity, I decided to food buff then run through to exit the cave (only later realising I could have hearthstoned out). That was the end of that character, and the end of me playing hardcore/wow for quite some time.

> A new age of dying, then respawning!

Recently after watching some Josh Strife Hayes, and having quite a rough time with work, I decided to get back into playing World of Warcraft again. This time I would not play such a punishing game mode, instead opting for a slower levelling and gameplay experience that didn't put me all the way back to the beginning if I was playing tired or not focussing properly every fight.

Even more luckily, I had a friend join me after I started playing a couple days in, and he has since been the healer to my warrior, making the whole experience much more enjoyable. I have still had that axe in the back of my mind all this time, waiting till I levelled up enough where I would be able to take on the quest.

> Sunder, the badass warrior

Not only was it in the back of my mind, but after mentioning it to my lvl 70 Warrior buddy, he excitedly explained just how good of a damage buff it was, to the point I should repec my character to get sweeping strikes just to buff it even more. He also explained that cleave has AoE (which I wish I had known earlier!) as I had been only using heroic strike on everything up until that point (still viable on single targets, but def love the AoE for keeping up threat against groups!.

Sunder even mentioned he would be more than happy to buy me the materials and help me to complete the quest once I hit level 30 as it was something he himself still remembers fondly.

> Reaching level 30

Once I finally did reach level 30 though, Sunder was not online. We both live in different timezones, and I only have his character as a friend in WoW so not too sure when he would be on next, I started collecting the materials myself and investigating where I would need to go to complete the quest.

I found out we would need to travel down through Stranglethorn vale toward Booty Bay, then head over on a ship to a Horde location named Ratchet. Here I could do the quest out on a small island that would not only put me on the path toward the axe, but also unlock a new battle stance, Berserk which would help increase my damage.

Excited me and my friend ran our way down through the valley (narrowly missing several gorillas) and into Booty Bay. We sailed across to Ratchet with me barely making the boat, literally saw it pull up on the other side while we were near the gryphon master and I ran my little legs as fast as I could, it started moving just as I was jumping onto it!

> Reaching Ratchet

I had no idea where to go after hitting the horde side, my first time in Ratchet. Did a quick google for a video showing where to go, pointed me to an island just out past the landing dock, so made my way with my priest friend down through the horde side.

Definitely not used to being around so many Horde characters, having played on Alliance up until this point. Was funny seeing stormwind npcs being treated as the mobs for the area. I swam across to the island after reaching the bay it sat across from, with a fatigue bar slowly dropping down as I kept swimming across.

Once I hit the shallower shore though was pleased to see it fade out from site (was worried I was about to drown out in the middle of the ocean!).

> A Tauren Approaches

While walking over to now start completing my arena trials (taking on various level 23-28 NPCs) I noticed another player walk up, just outside the arena zone.

As I was clearing out the npcs, he started to try auto-attacking them before I landed my first hit, tagging them under himself. I started to get a bit worried the more he did this. He would keep backing out of the arena then rushing in to grab the npcs as soon as they joined the fight.

At first I thought he was helping me out, but the more this continued, I realised he was trying to grief my quest. Luckily the minor NPC's did not seem to matter who tagged them, but I realised this might not be the same for Big Will.

Hitting the part of the quest now where Big Will was to be summoned, I got myself ready in Battle Stance, hovering over the keybind for my macro /startattack. Big Will walked up quite slowly, and was luckily able to be selected as he was walking up before he entered an attack state.

I used this to my advantage, selecting Big Will, and spamming the ability, grabbing the tag as soon as he changed states. I saw the Tauren quickly run forwards to attack him once he Big Will changed to aggro, but it was too late, I had already managed to grab the tag off of him.

> Beserker Stance Unlocked

With my new Beserker stance, I now made my way back to the Eastern Kingdoms, and to where the Windwatcher stayed. We started his quest, I bought a bunch of items off the Auction House (with my mad action skills to get a good price), and then slayed Bloodscalp trolls to get the remaining tusks needed.

Heading back though, realised how strong a foe Windwatcher would be. I stopped off at Ironforge to grab some throwing weapons, in the hopes that I would be able to possibly kite the Windwatcher after he spawned, up and down the river.

I levelled up my throwing skill to the max at the time (I was level 33) while doing some questing with my friend in Hillsbrad Foothills.
As we made our way up to the quest I had one last look at my friends list in the hope Sunder had jumped back on (as he mentioned being able to help us out with his level 60), but alas, no luck.

As we neared Bath'rah the Windwatcher, a player standing nearby popped onto the screen. It was a level 50 Draenei, just sitting AFK beside the trainer.

Excited I asked them if they could help out, explaining my situation and how close I was to getting the axe. No answer, just continued staring into the void.

I decided to send them a whisper as well, just incase they jumped back online. My and my priest friend then practiced walking up and down the river, just incase we had to use out kiting method.

> User Online

Luckily for the both of us, I got a ding, and saw a message back from the Draenei. "Invite me". I ran over quickly and sent through the group invite. They joined almost immediately and I started the Cyclonian summon, activating the windwatcher.

The NPC took us down near the shore, and he began to call Cyclonian out, all of a sudden with many gusts of wind, Cyclonian sprang out of thin air, and started attacking. The Draenei began tanking, taking on this new threat. My friend kept up the healing on him, and I did my pitiful amounts of damage on Cyclonian as well, being careful not to accidentally tank any hits.

Multiple time Cyclonian threw this level 50 up into the air, high above our heads. I got really worried he might just turn around and start attacking us but luckily this was not the case.

After taking him down, we thanked the Draenei who then road off into the sunset. I turned in my now complete quest and chose the Whirlwind axe as my reward!

Really enjoyed this quest. Was quite a long journey, but still enjoy using my axe even now at level 40! (hoping to maybe get it enchanted with Fiery Weapon)
