# The First Mount

Reaching the point where a character finally earns a mount is one of the cleanest progression moments in Classic. Before that, every trip feels long. Every errand across two zones asks for patience. Then suddenly the game gives a little of your time back.

## The Gold Grind

The real challenge was never the trainer. It was the cost. I sold everything that was not nailed down, cut back on impulse purchases, and started looking at quest rewards less like upgrades and more like coin waiting to happen.

Grinding the final stretch taught me more about discipline than any single quest chain. Bags filled with vendor trash became meaningful, and even small upgrades had to justify what they cost.

## The Moment It Paid Off

When the mount was finally purchased, the change was immediate. Roads that used to feel like chores became part of the reward. Returning to a city no longer meant planning around the run back. It just meant riding.

## Why It Matters

The first mount is memorable because it marks a shift in how Azeroth feels. The world does not get smaller, but it becomes more manageable. That small difference changes everything:

- Quest routes become smoother.
- Gold farming becomes faster.
- Every future trip feels just a little more heroic.

For a warrior especially, it feels less like buying convenience and more like earning momentum.
